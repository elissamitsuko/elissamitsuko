Impact of shading and cell variance on PV module power

ARENA and the Asia Pacific Solar Research Conference
2016
Simulation results of the impact of non-uniform irradiance for bi-facial modules on energy yield.
Real-World Impact:
• Recognised by Australian Renewable Energy Agency (ARENA)
• Models help predict the power output of bifacial modules under different conditions, considering the complex interaction of front and rear illumination
• The models guide the deployment of bifacial systems by identifying optimal configurations and installation parameters to minimise mismatch losses
• The development of these models contributes to standard procedures for testing and mitigating mismatch issues in bifacial modules

Environmental Risk Assessment of Alkaline Electrolyser’s Global Value Chain Actors

Nova University of Lisbon, University College Dublin, Poznan University of Economics and Business, Donau University Krems
2024
This study provides a global overview of the biodiversity and water-related risks associated with key actors in the Global Value Chain (GVC) of alkaline electrolysers (ALK), one of the most mature pieces of equipment for advancing green hydrogen production.
Applied Impact:
• The unequal distribution of risks and benefits between the Global South (resource extraction) and Global North (technology deployment) underscores the need for a just energy transition
• Environmental and climate risks vary across GVC stages, requiring tailored strategies such as adaptation in mining regions and water efficiency in deployment regions
• Greater transparency, inclusive data, and community engagement are critical to reducing risks and ensuring legitimacy in the global green hydrogen sector
